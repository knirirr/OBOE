[job.type]: Land-Cover Change

Copyright © [job.copyright]

 

Land-cover and land-use are changing at unprecedented rates in response to the combined effects of climate change, the pressures of human population growth, and changes in economic and socio-political dynamics. These changes are not, on the whole, thought favourable, though we can hope for a new era of more effective stewardship1. Monitoring, analysing and understanding land-cover change phenomena in this context is already crucial to the long-term security and wellbeing of society:  phenomena like a) tropical deforestation, b) agricultural expansion and abandonment, c) increasing frequency of flooding and water-logging, d) dust-bowling—vegetation loss and desertification, e) habitat fragmentation and the  consequent threat to biodiversity, f) salt-marshing and brackish-flooding of coastal lands, g) denudation of water catchments and the consequent loss of water quality,  h) bush encroachment on savannah and grassland habitats, and i) urbanisation/de-urbanisation, are just a few among many in the ensemble of land-cover changes affecting people, the economy, and society’s ability to secure its future.

The global security of food and agriculture is coming under increasingly severe threat from environmental change and a growing demand for food by an expanding population1. How society is able to respond to these threats will be determined, at least in part, by our understanding of how land cover, and ultimately land use, is changing in response to increasing pressures.

 

The GlobCover project of the European Space Agency (http://due.esrin.esa.int/globcover/) has produced the highest resolution (300m) global land cover classification yet available.  This brief introduction to the project appears on the ESA portal web site (09-FEB-2013):

GlobCover is an ESA initiative which began in 2005 in partnership with JRC, EEA, FAO, UNEP, GOFC-GOLD and IGBP. The aim of the project was to develop a service capable of delivering global composites and land cover maps using as input observations from the 300m MERIS sensor on board the ENVISAT satellite mission. ESA makes available the land cover maps, which cover 2 periods: December 2004 - June 2006 and January - December 2009.

See Bicheron et al. (2008) and Bontemps et al. (2011) for complete product description and validation reports.

Also note that these products are not without problematic errors and, as noted by the authors, they cannot be used for any kind of change detection because the expected rate of change is lower than the compounded error rate of the classification differences.

Last but not least, it is worth mentioning that the GlobCover 2009 land cover map cannot be used for any change detection application. In particular, the direct comparison with the previous GlobCover 2005 product cannot be done since it cannot guarantee accurate land cover change quantification. […]The GlobCover maps (both 2005 and 2009) are provided as they are with a quantified error. Their accuracy surely prevents any consistent comparison with older maps to depict the change area since the change rate will always be much lower than the classification errors, thus hampering any relevant use for change mapping.

Bontemps  et al., 2011. (p. 48).

This document describes the output of an automated data extraction and analysis service (OBOE-LCC) that acts on the GlobCover data products that have been made available to the public by ESA.

Some of the analyses that follow do show changes occurring between 2005 and 2009, but the user here is reminded of the caveat quoted above. We are involved in a number of projects to address the low confidence in global land cover change detection and the user is invited to contact us for further information.

The goals of the OBOE-LCC service are:

1.

To provide a data extraction facility whereby user selected sub-regions of the GlobCover products are delivered to users for reuse in further analyses.

2.

To provide data summaries (in tables and spread sheets) and visualisations (as high quality maps and legends) of user selected sub-regions of the GlobCover products.

3.

To provide extended analyses of land cover in the selected sub-regions.

4.

To solicit from users feedback in the form of expert commentaries and analyses of land cover and land-cover change in the selected sub-regions.

5.

To facilitate further research and publication on land cover and land-cover change.
